A Power Series about $x_{0}$ has the general form:

$$\sum_{k=0}^\infty c_{k}(x-x_{0})^k$$
where the $c_{k}$ are called the coefficients of the power series.

To determine for which values of x a power series converges, apply the Ratio Test:
$$|\frac{a_{k+1}}{a_{k}}|=|\frac{c_{k+1}(x-x_{0})^{k+1}}{c_{k}(x-x_{0})^k}|$$
$$=|\frac{c_{k+!}}{c_{k}}||x-x_{0}|$$
By the Ratio Test, this converges if and only if $lim_{k\rightarrow \infty}|\frac{c_{k+1}}{c_k}| |x-x_{0}|<1$ or $|x-x_0| < lim_{k\rightarrow \infty }|\frac{c_{k}}{c_{k+1}}|$

We call the value $lim_{k\rightarrow \infty}|\frac{c_k}{c_{k+1}}|$ the radius of convergence R
